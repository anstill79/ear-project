How to use ListeningCheck

Overview

ListeningCheck plays audio at different volumes to use when adjusting hearing devices or doing any other "how does this sound" exercises.

Playing audio

Click any numbered button (0–10) to play the selected audio file at that volume. The number represents relative loudness — 10 is full volume, 0 is silent.

Click the active button again (or press space bar) to stop it. You will need to adjust your computer or phone's volume to find the right range for testing. Use the Stop All button or press Enter / Esc to stop all audio at once.

Two columns

Buttons in the second column will fill downward if the first column's level was louder than the second column's.

Column headers are editable — click to rename them.

Checkbox indicator

The small checkbox to the right of each button marks a level you want to reference. Click it to toggle. Only one per column can be marked at a time.

Loop

Enable Loop to repeat the audio continuously. Choose Forever, 5 seconds, or 10 seconds to control how long it loops before stopping.

Keyboard shortcuts

Enter / Esc/ Space Stop all audio
