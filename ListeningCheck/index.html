How to use ListeningCheck

Overview

ListeningCheck plays audio at different volumes so you can find your hearing threshold — the quietest level you can still detect sound.

Playing audio

Click any numbered button (0–10) to play the selected audio file at that volume. The number represents relative loudness — 10 is full volume, 0 is silent.

Click the active button again to stop it. Buttons fill downward to show which levels you've tested.

Two columns

Use Level One to establish a reference volume you can clearly hear. Then use Level Two to find your threshold — the gap between them is highlighted in green, showing the range you're testing across.

Column headers are editable — click to rename them.

Heard indicator

The small checkbox to the right of each button marks whether you heard that level. Click it to toggle. Only one per column can be marked at a time.

Loop

Enable Loop to repeat the audio continuously. Choose Forever, 5 seconds, or 10 seconds to control how long it loops before stopping.
